Module 1

Definitions

  • Music - The organization of Sound and Silence
  • Beat - Steady thing that propels music forward; Helps organize the music
  • Rhythm - The beat within the beat; More rhythmic and not necessarily steady
  • Pitch - The relative highness or lowness of sound
    • Size Pitch relationship - Big stuff is low and Small stuff is high
    • Notes - ABCDEFG
    • Interval - The distance between two notes (in pitch)
    • Range - How high and low an instrument can go
  • Dynamics - How loud or soft something is played
    • p - piano - soft
    • f - forte - hard
    • m (used before p or f) - mezzo - medium
    • Crescendo - gets louder (<)
    • Decrescendo - gets quieter (>)
    • Subito - sudden change (in loudness)
  • Timbre - Color of sound (Same note on different instruments sound different)
  • Melody:
    • Melodic Contour - Shape of the Melody
      • Conjunct - Nice and wavy; Gradual; Big jump followed by small steps
        • Most things are made this way
      • Disjunct - Very sharp and all over the place
        • Hakan Hardenberger - Hans werner Henze - Sonatina
  • Harmony - Provides narrative drive; Propulsion; Where you go
  • Texture:
    • Monophonic - One voice; One melodic line; No harmony
    • Homophonic - One melodic line with other things; "With harmony and stuff" - Zelinski
    • Polyphonic - Many voices; More than one melodic like at the same time
